Welcome to “Sites That Do It Right”! This website will take you through some necessary considerations everyone should make when they create a website. Accessibility is too often overlooked, so I’ll be outlining some principles that make internet surfing easier for our diverse population.

ACCESSIBILITY

INTO DESIGN

Integrating accessibility into design

Making your website accessible at the most basic level is easier than you might think! Integrating accessibility can be as simple as:

  • Finding a dyslexia friendly font
  • Adding alternative text to images
  • Or separating your content with descriptive headings

We’ll take a look at how to do these, among other important things, don’t worry.

ACCESSIBILITY

IMPORTANT?

Why is accessibility important?

It blows my mind how many organisations fail to consider how they could be making their products or services more accessible. First of all, a huge chunk of the population misses out on experiencing their service which, from a business perspective, is a huge loss. More importantly, you never know which members of your target audience will need the assistance.

Accessibility considerations don’t just apply to someone who is disabled; creating a website that you can navigate with a keyboard would help someone who has a broken arm the same as it would help someone who has never had use of their arms.

You never know who might need it.
